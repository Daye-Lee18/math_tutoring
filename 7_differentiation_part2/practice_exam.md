# Differentiation Part 2 — Exam Problems

---

**1** &emsp; A curve has the equation

$$x^2(2 + y) - y^2 = 0.$$

Find an expression for $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$ in terms of $x$ and $y$. &emsp; **(6)**

---

**2** &emsp; A curve has the equation

$$4x^2 - 2xy - y^2 + 11 = 0.$$

Find an equation for the normal to the curve at the point with coordinates $(-1,\ -3)$. &emsp; **(8)**

---

**3** &emsp; *(a)* &emsp; Prove that

$$\frac{d}{dx}(a^x) = a^x \ln a. \tag{3}$$

A curve has the equation $y = 4^x - 2^{x-1} + 1$.

&emsp; *(b)* &emsp; Show that the tangent to the curve at the point where it crosses the $y$-axis has the equation

$$3x\ln 2 - 2y + 3 = 0. \tag{5}$$

&emsp; *(c)* &emsp; Find the exact coordinates of the stationary point of the curve. &emsp; **(4)**

---

**4** &emsp; A curve has the equation

$$2x^2 + xy - y^2 + 18 = 0.$$

Find the coordinates of the points where the tangent to the curve is parallel to the $x$-axis. &emsp; **(8)**

---

**5** &emsp; Figure 2 shows the curve with parametric equations

$$x = -1 + 4\cos\theta, \qquad y = 2\sqrt{2}\sin\theta, \qquad 0 \leq \theta < 2\pi.$$

The point $P$ on the curve has coordinates $(1,\ \sqrt{6})$.

&emsp; *(a)* &emsp; Find the value of $\theta$ at $P$. &emsp; **(2)**

&emsp; *(b)* &emsp; Show that the normal to the curve at $P$ passes through the origin. &emsp; **(7)**

&emsp; *(c)* &emsp; Find a Cartesian equation for the curve. &emsp; **(3)**

---

**6** &emsp; A curve has parametric equations

$$x = 3\cos^2 t, \qquad y = \sin 2t, \qquad 0 \leq t < \pi.$$

&emsp; *(a)* &emsp; Show that $\dfrac{dy}{dx} = -\dfrac{2}{3}\cot 2t$. &emsp; **(4)**

&emsp; *(b)* &emsp; Find the coordinates of the points where the tangent to the curve is parallel to the $x$-axis. &emsp; **(3)**

&emsp; *(c)* &emsp; Show that the tangent to the curve at the point where $t = \dfrac{\pi}{6}$ has the equation

$$2x + 3\sqrt{3}\,y = 9. \tag{3}$$

&emsp; *(d)* &emsp; Find a Cartesian equation for the curve in the form $y^2 = f(x)$. &emsp; **(4)**
