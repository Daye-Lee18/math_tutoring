## Question 1 &nbsp;&nbsp; (7 marks)

$z = 4-3i$, $w = 1+2i$

### Part (a) — 3 marks

$z+w = (4+1)+(-3+2)i = \boxed{5-i}$ &emsp; **B1**

$z-w = (4-1)+(-3-2)i = \boxed{3-5i}$ &emsp; **B1**

$$zw = (4-3i)(1+2i) = 4+8i-3i-6i^2 = 4+5i+6 = \boxed{10+5i} \quad \textbf{B1}$$

### Part (b) — 3 marks

Multiply numerator and denominator by conjugate of $w$: &emsp; **M1**

$$\frac{z}{w} = \frac{4-3i}{1+2i}\times\frac{1-2i}{1-2i} = \frac{4-8i-3i+6i^2}{1-4i^2} \quad \textbf{M1}$$

$$= \frac{4-11i-6}{1+4} = \frac{-2-11i}{5}$$

$$\boxed{= -\frac{2}{5} - \frac{11}{5}i} \quad \textbf{A1}$$

### Part (c) — 1 mark

$$\boxed{z^* = 4+3i} \quad \textbf{B1}$$

## Question 2 &nbsp;&nbsp; (5 marks)

Let $\sqrt{5+12i} = a+bi$ where $a, b \in \mathbb{R}$. Square both sides: &emsp; **M1**

$$5+12i = (a+bi)^2 = (a^2-b^2)+2abi \quad \textbf{M1}$$

Compare real and imaginary parts: &emsp; **M1**

$$a^2 - b^2 = 5 \qquad 2ab = 12 \implies b = \frac{6}{a}$$

Substitute: $a^2 - \dfrac{36}{a^2} = 5 \implies a^4 - 5a^2 - 36 = 0$ &emsp; **M1**

$$(a^2-9)(a^2+4) = 0 \implies a^2 = 9 \quad (a^2 = -4 \text{ rejected, } a \in \mathbb{R})$$

$a = 3 \Rightarrow b = 2$ &ensp; or &ensp; $a = -3 \Rightarrow b = -2$

$$\boxed{\sqrt{5+12i} = 3+2i \quad \text{or} \quad -3-2i} \quad \textbf{A1}$$

## Question 3 &nbsp;&nbsp; (6 marks)

### Part (a) — 4 marks

**$z_1 = -1+\sqrt{3}\,i$** — point $(-1,\sqrt{3})$, 2nd quadrant:

$$|z_1| = \sqrt{(-1)^2+(\sqrt{3})^2} = \sqrt{4} = \boxed{2} \quad \textbf{B1}$$

$$\alpha = \tan^{-1}\!\!\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{1}\right) = \frac{\pi}{3} \implies \arg(z_1) = \pi - \frac{\pi}{3} = \boxed{\frac{2\pi}{3}} \quad \textbf{M1 A1}$$

**$z_2 = \sqrt{3}-i$** — point $(\sqrt{3},-1)$, 4th quadrant:

$$|z_2| = \sqrt{3+1} = \boxed{2} \quad \textbf{B1}$$

$$\alpha = \tan^{-1}\!\!\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\right) = \frac{\pi}{6} \implies \arg(z_2) = \boxed{-\frac{\pi}{6}} \quad \textbf{A1}$$

### Part (b) — 2 marks

$$\left|\frac{z_1}{z_2}\right| = \frac{|z_1|}{|z_2|} = \frac{2}{2} = \boxed{1} \quad \textbf{B1}$$

$$\arg\!\left(\frac{z_1}{z_2}\right) = \arg(z_1) - \arg(z_2) = \frac{2\pi}{3} - \left(-\frac{\pi}{6}\right) = \frac{4\pi}{6}+\frac{\pi}{6} = \boxed{\frac{5\pi}{6}} \quad \textbf{A1}$$

(Check: $\dfrac{5\pi}{6}$ is in the range $(-\pi, \pi]$ ✓)

## Question 4 &nbsp;&nbsp; (7 marks)

### Part (a) — 3 marks

$$R = |z| = \sqrt{1^2+1^2} = \sqrt{2} \quad \textbf{B1}$$

Point $(1,1)$ is in 1st quadrant: $\theta = \tan^{-1}\!\!\left(\dfrac{1}{1}\right) = \dfrac{\pi}{4}$ &emsp; **M1**

$$\boxed{z = \sqrt{2}\,e^{i\pi/4}} \quad \textbf{A1}$$

### Part (b) — 4 marks

$$z^8 = \left(\sqrt{2}\,e^{i\pi/4}\right)^8 = (\sqrt{2})^8\cdot e^{i\cdot 8\pi/4} \quad \textbf{M1}$$

$$= 2^4\cdot e^{2\pi i} \quad \textbf{M1}$$

$$e^{2\pi i} = \cos 2\pi + i\sin 2\pi = 1 + 0i \quad \textbf{M1}$$

$$z^8 = 16\times 1 = \boxed{16}$$

Since Im$(z^8) = 0$, $z^8$ is a positive real number. $\quad\square \quad \textbf{A1}$

## Question 6 &nbsp;&nbsp; (8 marks)

$u = 1+2i$

### Part (a) — 2 marks

$$u^2 = (1+2i)^2 = 1 + 4i + 4i^2 = 1+4i-4 = -3+4i \quad \square \quad \textbf{B1 B1}$$

(B1 for expanding correctly, B1 for $i^2 = -1$ applied correctly to reach $-3+4i$)

### Part (b) — 3 marks

$$|u^2| = \sqrt{(-3)^2+4^2} = \sqrt{9+16} = \sqrt{25} = \boxed{5} \quad \textbf{B1}$$

Point $(-3,4)$ is in 2nd quadrant: &emsp; **M1**

$$\alpha = \tan^{-1}\!\!\left(\frac{4}{3}\right) = 0.9273\ldots \implies \arg(u^2) = \pi - 0.93 = \boxed{2.21\text{ rad}} \quad \textbf{A1}$$

### Part (c) — 3 marks

Coefficients $a, b$ are real, so complex roots occur in conjugate pairs: &emsp; **M1**

The other root is $u^* = 1-2i$.

Sum of roots $= (1+2i)+(1-2i) = 2 = -a \implies \boxed{a = -2}$ &emsp; **A1**

Product of roots $= (1+2i)(1-2i) = 1+4 = 5 = b \implies \boxed{b = 5}$ &emsp; **A1**
