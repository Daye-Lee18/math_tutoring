**1** &nbsp; Relative to a fixed origin, two lines have the equations

$$\mathbf{r} = (7\mathbf{j} - 4\mathbf{k}) + s(4\mathbf{i} - 3\mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k})$$

and

$$\mathbf{r} = (-7\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} + 8\mathbf{k}) + t(-3\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{k})$$

where $s$ and $t$ are scalar parameters.

&emsp;*(a)* &ensp; Show that the two lines intersect and find the position vector of the point where they meet. **(5)**

&emsp;*(b)* &ensp; Find, in degrees to 1 decimal place, the acute angle between the lines. **(4)**

**2** &nbsp; The points $A$ and $B$ have coordinates $(3,\,9,\,-7)$ and $(13,\,-6,\,-2)$ respectively.

&emsp;*(a)* &ensp; Find, in vector form, an equation for the line $l$ which passes through $A$ and $B$. **(2)**

&emsp;*(b)* &ensp; Show that the point $C$ with coordinates $(9,\,0,\,-4)$ lies on $l$. **(2)**

The point $D$ is the point on $l$ closest to the origin $O$.

&emsp;*(c)* &ensp; Find the coordinates of $D$. **(4)**

&emsp;*(d)* &ensp; Find the area of triangle $OAB$ to 3 significant figures. **(3)**

**3** &nbsp; Relative to a fixed origin $O$, the line $l$ has the equation

$$\mathbf{r} = (\mathbf{i} + p\mathbf{j} - 5\mathbf{k}) + \lambda(3\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} + q\mathbf{k})$$

where $p$ and $q$ are constants and $\lambda$ is a scalar parameter.

Given that the point $A$ with coordinates $(-5,\,9,\,-9)$ lies on $l$,

&emsp;*(a)* &ensp; find the values of $p$ and $q$. **(3)**

&emsp;*(b)* &ensp; show that the point $B$ with coordinates $(25,\,-1,\,11)$ also lies on $l$. **(2)**

The point $C$ lies on $l$ and is such that $OC$ is perpendicular to $l$.

&emsp;*(c)* &ensp; Find the coordinates of $C$. **(4)**

&emsp;*(d)* &ensp; Find the ratio $AC : CB$. **(2)**

**4** &nbsp; A straight road passes through villages at the points $A$ and $B$ with position vectors $(9\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k})$ and $(4\mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k})$ respectively, relative to a fixed origin.

The road ends at a junction at the point $C$ with another straight road which lies along the line with equation

$$\mathbf{r} = (2\mathbf{i} + 16\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}) + \mu(-5\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j})$$

where $\mu$ is a scalar parameter.

&emsp;*(a)* &ensp; Find the position vector of $C$. **(5)**

Given that 1 unit on each coordinate axis represents 200 metres,

&emsp;*(b)* &ensp; find the distance, in kilometres, from the village at $A$ to the junction at $C$. **(4)**

**5** &nbsp; Relative to a fixed origin, two lines have the equations

$$\mathbf{r} = \begin{pmatrix}4\\1\\1\end{pmatrix} + s\begin{pmatrix}1\\4\\5\end{pmatrix} \qquad \text{and} \qquad \mathbf{r} = \begin{pmatrix}-3\\1\\-6\end{pmatrix} + t\begin{pmatrix}3\\a\\b\end{pmatrix}$$

where $a$ and $b$ are constants and $s$ and $t$ are scalar parameters.

Given that the two lines are perpendicular,

&emsp;*(a)* &ensp; find a linear relationship between $a$ and $b$. **(2)**

Given also that the two lines intersect,

&emsp;*(b)* &ensp; find the values of $a$ and $b$. **(8)**

&emsp;*(c)* &ensp; find the coordinates of the point where they intersect. **(2)**
